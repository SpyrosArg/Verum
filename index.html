01 What it does

A seal placed before
the AI sees anything.

The moment data arrives, before any model, any agent, any pipeline touches it, verum takes a cryptographic fingerprint and binds it to a timestamp.

That fingerprint travels with every AI decision as a compact receipt. It cannot be separated from the decision. It cannot be backdated.

Later, a second later, a year later, in a regulatory audit, anyone with the receipt and the original data can verify: did the AI act on this exact data, unaltered?

Match means genuine. Mismatch means something changed.

Step 01

→ verum.seal() called here
Step 02

SHA3-256 fingerprint · timestamp · nonce
→ AI processes normally
Step 03

Step 04
Receipt issued
decision + seal · tamper-evident · verifiable forever
02 How it works

Three functions.
Standard library only.

01
seal(data, source_id)
Fingerprints raw input using SHA3-256 with a nanosecond timestamp and a cryptographic nonce. Two calls on identical data always produce different seals — the nonce guarantees uniqueness.
02
bind(seal, decision)
Chains the seal to the AI decision, producing a compact receipt. The decision and the input proof are cryptographically inseparable. One cannot be presented without the other.
03
verify(receipt, data)
Recomputes the fingerprint from the claimed original data and compares it against the seal using timing-safe comparison. Returns a typed result with a boolean and a human-readable reason.
example.py — pip install verum
from verum import seal, bind, verify

# before the AI sees anything
s = seal(data="your raw input data here")

# after the AI decides
receipt = bind(seal=s, decision="ai decision output here")

# verify anytime — by anyone
result = verify(receipt=receipt, original_data="your raw input data here")

print(result.valid)      # True
print(result.reason)     # "input matches seal, timestamp intact"
03 Use cases

Anywhere AI acts
on live data.

01
AI agent pipelines
In multi-agent systems, one agent's output becomes the next agent's input. Seal every handoff. Each agent can prove it received exactly what was sent, creating a complete, auditable chain of custody across the entire pipeline.
→ seal every agent handoff
02
Space & critical infrastructure
AI systems in ground stations and control centers act on sensor and telemetry data. Verum provides a cryptographic record that the data the AI acted on was the genuine feed, not a replayed, delayed, or modified signal.
→ seal telemetry before AI processing
03
Healthcare & regulated industries
EU AI Act Article 12 requires high-risk AI systems to log their decisions. Verum extends that requirement to the input layer, proving the data the AI processed was the correct, unaltered record at the time of decision.
→ satisfy Article 12 at the input layer
04
Finance & algorithmic systems
Algorithmic systems act on market data, risk scores, and transaction records. Verum creates a verifiable record of the exact data state at the moment of every consequential decision, reproducible in any audit or dispute.
→ create audit-ready decision records
